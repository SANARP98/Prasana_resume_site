## OutlineCV: Site Flow & Design Analysis

### **Overall Purpose**
OutlineCV is a clean, minimalist portfolio/resume website template for a content-first professional (Sasha Mozdir, an editorial systems specialist and entrepreneur). Built with Framer, it showcases work through a narrative-driven approach rather than traditional CV format.

***

### **Site Architecture & Navigation Flow**

#### **Header Navigation (Persistent Across Pages)**
- **Social Links** (top-left): Framer portfolio, LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Instagram - all accessible via minimalist icon buttons
- **Primary CTA Buttons** (top-right):
  - "CV" button - downloads PDF resume
  - "HELLO@TMPL.DIGITAL" - email contact link

#### **Page Structure**

**1. HOME PAGE** (Landing/Main Profile)
The homepage follows a content-first, narrative approach with clear section hierarchy:

- **Profile Introduction**
  - Circular profile photo (neon yellow-green accent)
  - Name: "Sasha Mozdir"
  - Title: "ENTREPRENEUR"
  - Personal bio with linked keywords (Chemimedia agency, locations: Georgia, Canada, US)
  - Tone: Conversational and direct

- **Educational Background Section (STUDY)**
  - Philosophy & Design degree from Parsons
  - Dual degree from Voldsmiths, University of London
  - Content architecture training at Cyper Island
  - Uses emoji badges for visual distinction

- **Professional Experience Section (EXPERIENCE)**
  - 3 highlighted roles: Coole, Plitch, Sportics
  - Brief role descriptions with linked company names
  - Emphasis on content strategy and systems work
  - Features inline tags (e.g., "storytelling" as a hyperlink)

- **Tools & Skills Section (INFO)**
  - Lists tools used: Notion, Framer, Figma, SEO platforms, ChatGPT
  - Explains work philosophy and approach

- **Case Studies Showcase (CASES)**
  - Grid of 4 featured case studies displayed with:
    - Colorful icon backgrounds (dark gray, pink, purple, cyan, red, lime)
    - White asterisk symbol in each icon
    - Case title (clickable)
    - Date (MM/DD/YY format)
    - Project type or company name (NDA badges for confidential work)
  - "ALL" button leads to full cases archive

- **Footer**
  - Copyright notice
  - "ALL CASES" link (central)
  - "MADE BY" link to template creator

***

**2. CASES PAGE** (/cases)
A dedicated archive showcasing all case studies in a vertical list format:

- **Navigation**: "HOME" back link at top
- **Page Title**: "Cases"
- **List Format**:
  - All cases displayed as clickable items
  - Same visual design as homepage (colored icon + title + date + client/type)
  - Cases visible on initial load: 4 items
  - "LOAD MORE" button reveals additional cases
  - After expansion: 8+ cases visible including:
    - 300 Articles Rebuilt, 1,500+ Illustrations Created (Sportics)
    - Localizing a Blog Across 5 Languages (SAAS)
    - Building a Semi-Automated Blog with AI + Human Review (Prody)
    - Turning a Corporate Blog Into a Brand Statement (CMS13)

***

**3. CASE STUDY DETAIL PAGE** (/cases/[case-slug])
Individual case pages follow a structured narrative format:

- **Navigation**: "HOME" back link
- **Header**:
  - Large, clear title
  - Date and NDA/client info
  
- **Body Content Structure**:
  - **Opening Summary**: Context and scope of work (1-2 paragraphs)
  - **"What I did"** - Bulleted list of deliverables:
    - Specific deliverables and implementations
    - Tools used (e.g., "Structured a modular blog using Framer CMS")
  - **"Why it matters"** - Business impact and outcomes
  - **Results** - Tangible achievements with metrics

- **Related Cases Section**: Lists 4 other cases for navigation
- **Footer**: Links to all cases and template info

***

### **Visual Design Principles**

**Color System**:
- Minimalist neutral baseline (white background, dark text)
- Vibrant accent colors for case study icons:
  - Dark Gray, Pink, Purple, Cyan, Red, Lime Green
  - Each case gets a distinct color for visual scanning
- Emoji badges (ðŸ”’ NDA, âœ¨ checkmarks) for quick status identification

**Typography**:
- Clean, readable sans-serif (appears to be system font or neutral web-safe)
- Clear hierarchy: Large titles â†’ section headers â†’ body text
- Generous whitespace between sections
- Inline links colored in context (blue for clickable text)

**Layout**:
- Single-column layout (mobile-first responsive design)
- Maximum line length ~65-70 characters for readability
- Consistent 16-24px spacing between sections
- Minimal UI elements - no sidebars or complex navigation

**Interactive Elements**:
- Subtle hover states on links (implied by design)
- Loading spinner animation (turquoise circle) when navigating between pages
- "LOAD MORE" button for progressive content disclosure
- Framer badge in bottom-right (template attribution)

### **User Flow Patterns**

1. **Discovery Flow**:
   - Land on homepage
   - Read profile/bio
   - Scan featured cases
   - Click "ALL" to explore full case library

2. **Case Study Deep-Dive**:
   - Browse cases list
   - Click specific case
   - Read detailed narrative
   - See related cases
   - Navigate to another case or back to list

3. **Contact/Action Flow**:
   - Email icon in header
   - CV download option
   - Social media links at top

***

### **Key Design Strengths**

- **Content-First**: No visual fluff; pure focus on work and narrative
- **Accessibility**: High contrast, readable typography, logical structure
- **Portfolio Narrative**: Cases tell a story rather than just listing projects
- **Template Focus**: Designed by tmpl.lemonsqueezy.com (template creator), so it serves as both portfolio AND product showcase
- **Minimal but Polished**: Professional yet approachable
- **Mobile-Optimized**: Single-column layout works well on all screen sizes
- **Fast Navigation**: Clear pathways between pages; no complex menu systems

***

### **Technical Implementation**
- **Built with**: Framer (no-code website builder)
- **Deployment**: Framer-hosted (outlinecv.framer.website)
- **CMS Integration**: Framer CMS used for case management
- **Performance**: Optimized Framer site with lazy-loading transitions
